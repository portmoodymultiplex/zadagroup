Small-Scale Multi-Unit Housing

What SSMUH means for your lot.

The legislation that quietly changed what BC homeowners can build.

SSMUH stands for Small-Scale Multi-Unit Housing. In late 2023, the BC government passed legislation requiring most municipalities to allow more units on lots that used to be restricted to a single home. The rollout happened through 2024, and the practical effect for homeowners has been significant. But the conversation about it has stayed surprisingly quiet outside of planning circles.

The short version: in most BC municipalities with a population of 5,000 or more, lots that were previously zoned for one home can now accommodate three to four units. In areas served by frequent transit, that number can go up to six. The exact rules vary by municipality, but the floor is set provincially.

Why this matters for you.

If you own a home on a standard residential lot in Metro Vancouver, SSMUH almost certainly applies to you. That doesn't mean you should build. It means you have options you didn't have before. A few of the shapes that conversation can take:

  • Stay and add. Build a duplex or triplex on your existing lot, live in one, rent the others.
  • Co-develop. Partner with a builder or developer to redevelop your lot, share the proceeds, and downsize into one of the new units.
  • Sell to a builder. Sell your property to someone planning to develop it under SSMUH. Often this commands a premium over a traditional sale.
  • Sell as-is. The new zoning often increases the underlying land value, even if you do nothing.

The numbers that matter.

Whether any of this makes financial sense depends on the specifics: your lot size, your municipality's adopted bylaw, your existing mortgage, your tax situation, what you'd build, and what the market does between now and completion. We've spent significant time modeling these scenarios for clients, and the answer is different for almost every property.

SSMUH is not a yes-or-no. It is a question of, "let's look at the numbers for your lot specifically."
