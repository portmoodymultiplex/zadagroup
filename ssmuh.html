Small-Scale Multi-Unit Housing

What SSMUH means for your lot.

The legislation that quietly changed what BC homeowners can build.

SSMUH stands for Small-Scale Multi-Unit Housing. It's BC legislation from late 2023 (Bill 44) that requires most municipalities to allow more units on lots previously zoned for a single home. Most rollout happened through 2024.

The basic shape: in eligible BC municipalities (5,000+ population), lots that used to allow one home now allow three to four. Near frequent transit, up to six. Exact rules vary by city.

What it means for your lot.

If you own a residential lot in Metro Vancouver, SSMUH almost certainly applies. Your four practical options:

  • Stay and add. Build a duplex or triplex on your existing lot, live in one unit, rent the others.
  • Co-develop. Partner with a builder to redevelop. Share the proceeds. Downsize into one of the new units.
  • Sell to a builder. Often pays a premium over a traditional family-buyer sale.
  • Do nothing. The new zoning increases your land value regardless.

The math.

Whether building makes sense depends on your lot size, your municipality's bylaw, your mortgage, your tax situation, construction costs, and the market. It's a property-specific question. We model it lot by lot.
