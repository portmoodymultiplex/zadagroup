Port Moody Multiplex Initiative

Helping Port Moody residents see what's possible.

Education seminars, co-development planning, and a path to retirement income for longtime homeowners.

Port Moody is our neighbourhood. It's also a community where most homeowners have been on the same block for a long time. And where the new SSMUH and multiplex rules have created options that nobody is putting in front of them clearly.

Zada Group has been working in Port Moody to change that. We host education seminars, we sit down with neighbours one-on-one to model what the math actually looks like on their property, and we built a separate resource at portmoodymultiplex.ca, dedicated to walking residents through their options without the sales pitch.

What we're doing in Port Moody.

Community education seminars.

We've hosted multiple in-person seminars in Port Moody on what the multiplex and SSMUH rules mean for local homeowners. They're free, they're open to the community, and they're built to actually answer questions instead of generate leads. We're going to keep running them.

Downsizing without leaving the neighbourhood.

One of the most common Port Moody conversations: "I love this neighbourhood, but I can't manage this house anymore, and I don't want to move to Maple Ridge." Under the new rules, there's often a way to redevelop the existing lot into something smaller for you and something income-generating to fund the rest of your life. We help homeowners model that. What it would cost, what it would yield, what timeline it would take, and whether it makes sense for their specific situation.

Co-development support.

If you'd rather not handle the construction yourself, co-development with a builder is often the cleanest path. You contribute your land, the builder contributes the capital and the execution, and the proceeds (or units) get split according to a pre-agreed structure. We've helped clients navigate these conversations and can connect you with builders we trust.

Retirement income strategies.

For longtime owners with low or no mortgage, the new zoning can mean a path to consistent retirement income that wasn't on the table a few years ago. The most common version: one of the new units is yours to live in, the rest are rental income for the rest of your life. The math has to work, but when it does, it really does.

The point isn't to sell you something. It's to make sure you actually know what your lot can do.
