The Philosophy

We don't run a real estate practice that happens to do some community work. We run a community practice that happens to sell real estate.

For two decades, the operating principle has stayed the same. Look after the people first. The deals follow. 95% of our business comes from past clients and personal referrals. That isn't an accident. It's the only number we've ever actually worked toward.

So we spend almost nothing on advertising. No billboards, no bus benches, no Google ads chasing strangers. The marketing budget goes somewhere else entirely.

Where The Money Goes

Events. Gifts. The neighbourhood.

Carnivals open to the community. Private movie screenings for clients. Holiday mailouts that arrive in February, March, May, October. Welcome-home baskets on possession day. Closing gifts that took weeks to put together. We don't post most of it. This is some of it.

How We Show Up

The rituals.

None of this is a campaign. They're the things we do every year, on the same dates, because it's how we want to be in this community. Most of our clients can predict what's coming in the mailbox before it arrives.

Every December · 10+ Years

The Christmas gift.

Hand-delivered to past clients, every year, without fail. Always sourced from small Canadian businesses. The list of recipients has gotten longer every December for a decade and we've never once outsourced the delivery.

February

Valentines for the people who built the business.

A card and a chocolate bar to every past client. "You are the heart of our business" — and we mean it, because they are.

March

St. Patrick's Day scratch tickets.

"Lucky to have clients like you." A card, a Gold Rush ticket, and a reminder that we're still around. A few of our past clients have actually won.

Mother's Day

Coffee on Dawar.

We text every mom in the client database a Starbucks barcode for a free drink. Walk in, show your phone, the latte's on us. It started as a thank-you for one Mother's Day. It's now an annual thing people look forward to.

Fall

The community carnival.

Open to past clients, neighbours, and anyone who heard about it. Custom-labelled water bottles, mini doughnuts in branded bags, balloon arches, bouncy castles, a clown handing out balloons. The first one was big enough that we're now calling it annual.

December

Photos with Santa & private screenings.

Clients and friends bring their kids. Santa is real, the cookies are real, the photos are free. Some years we book out a movie theatre for a private screening just for clients. A reason to be in the same room with the people we work for, before the year ends.

When The Community Needs Us

We don't wait to be asked.

When something happens in our community, we move first and figure out the paperwork second. A few times that's mattered:

November 2021

Abbotsford flooding.

The atmospheric river hit and the Sumas Prairie was underwater within 48 hours. We donated immediately, before the news cycle had even caught up. Our clients in the Fraser Valley still talk about it.

March 2020

The first weeks of COVID.

When seniors' care homes locked down, we donated to local long-term care so the residents had something extra during the hardest stretch of the pandemic. We had family in that demographic. So did our clients.

Ongoing

Share Food Bank.

We've run a community fundraiser for Share Food Bank as part of one of our events, and the team has been quietly contributing for years. Hunger in our community is not something we're willing to be neutral about.

A 20-Year Commitment

Made in Canada. Sourced from small.

For more than two decades, every client gift, every event favour, every closing basket has been built around one rule: source it from a small Canadian business if at all possible. Local makers, family-run shops, BC vineyards, Canadian-owned brands.

It costs more. It takes longer. It's worth it. The dollars we spend on gifts move through the local economy instead of out of it, and the products tell the story of the country our clients are buying into.

The Team

One team lead. Two senior agents. Four languages. Zero ego.

Zada Group is built deliberately small. The team is led by Dawar Zada, PREC*, a Master Medallion Club REALTOR® with more than fifteen years in Metro Vancouver. Working alongside him are two senior agents who've been with Zada Group for years — Randy Quach, who works extensively with clients in law enforcement, government, and the military, and Liz Murphy, a New Westminster long-timer with international experience and a deep specialty in seniors' transitions.

Between us we speak English, French, Vietnamese, and Farsi. Every file is touched by more than one set of eyes. No one is ever the bottleneck. That's the entire structure, and it's structured that way on purpose.
