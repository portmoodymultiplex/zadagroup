For Buyers

The Zada Group Buyer's Guide.

No fluff. No filler. The seven things we wish every buyer knew before they started.

1. Know your real budget, not your pre-approval.

The bank will tell you what they're willing to lend. That number is not the same as what you should spend. Before you tour a single property, sit down with the actual monthly carrying cost (mortgage, property tax, strata fees, insurance, utilities, maintenance reserve) and decide what you're comfortable living with. The pre-approval is a ceiling, not a target.

2. Get the pre-approval before you fall in love.

Different lenders qualify you differently. Some will count rental income, some won't. Some will give you a better rate if you have specific banking products. Worth shopping around. And worth doing it before you find the place. Pre-approval letters expire (usually 90–120 days), so the timing matters.

3. Build a real short-list of what matters.

Three columns: must have, would be nice, dealbreaker. Be honest about which is which. The number of buyers we've seen overpay for a third bedroom they didn't actually need is not small.

4. Tour with a plan.

The third house always feels best. That's a quirk of fatigue and recency bias, not the houses. We try to tour in groups of three to five, with notes, and we revisit the top contender before any offer goes in.

5. Read the documents.

For a condo or townhouse: minutes of strata meetings, depreciation report, Form B, engaging documents. Boring, sometimes painful, always worth it. For a detached home: title, PDS, oil tank scan, permits, survey. The deal is in the documents. If your agent isn't reading them with you, you've got the wrong agent.

6. The offer is more than the price.

Conditions, deposit, completion date, possession date, subject removal period, included chattels. All of these are levers. Sometimes a faster completion wins over a higher price. Sometimes a longer subject period costs you the deal. Strategy is the part that earns the commission.

7. After the offer is accepted, the real work starts.

Inspection, financing finalization, insurance, lawyer, title transfer, walkthrough, possession. There are roughly 40 things that need to happen between "yes" and "keys" and we run a tight checklist for each of them. You should feel informed at every step. If you don't, ask.

The best buyer's agent makes you feel like you knew what was happening the whole time.
