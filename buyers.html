For Buyers

Buyer's Guide.

What to do, in order, when you're buying in Metro Vancouver.

1. Set your real budget.

Add up the actual monthly cost: mortgage, property tax, strata, insurance, utilities, maintenance. Decide what you can live with. That number, not the pre-approval, is your budget.

2. Get pre-approved.

Talk to two or three lenders before you start looking. Rates and qualification rules vary. Pre-approvals expire in 90 to 120 days.

3. Write down what you actually want.

Three columns: must-have, nice-to-have, dealbreaker. Bring it to your first meeting with us.

4. Tour with a plan.

Three to five homes per session. Take notes. Revisit the top one before you write an offer.

5. Read the documents before the offer.

Condo or townhouse: strata minutes, depreciation report, Form B, bylaws. Detached: title, PDS, permits, oil tank scan, survey if available. We go through these with you line by line.

6. Structure the offer.

Price is one piece. Subject removal period, deposit, completion and possession dates, included chattels — these all matter. We build the whole structure to fit your situation.

7. Subject removal and closing.

Inspection, financing, insurance, lawyer, title transfer, walkthrough, keys. We track it on a checklist so nothing slips.

First-time buyer?

Bring every question. There are no dumb ones at this stage.
